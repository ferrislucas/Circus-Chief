# Trademark Policy

This policy describes how the names, logos, and brand assets for Circus Chief
may be used. It is intended to clarify the trademark reservation already
recognized by the Apache License 2.0.

This policy is not a trademark registration notice and is not legal advice.

## Protected Marks

The protected marks include:

- Circus Chief
- Circus Time
- The Circus Chief logo and related visual brand assets
- Any confusingly similar names, logos, or brand presentations

The Apache License 2.0 grants broad copyright and patent permissions for the
software, but it does not grant permission to use the project's trade names,
trademarks, service marks, or product names except as needed to describe the
origin of the software.

## Permitted Uses

You may use the protected marks without separate permission when the use is
truthful, non-confusing, and limited to identifying the project. Examples
include:

- Saying that your project is based on Circus Chief.
- Linking to the Circus Chief repository or website.
- Referring to compatibility with Circus Chief.
- Including required copyright, license, attribution, or NOTICE information.

These uses must not imply that your project, product, service, package, fork,
add-on, or company is endorsed by, sponsored by, or affiliated with Circus
Chief unless you have written permission.

## Restricted Uses

You may not use the protected marks in a way that is likely to confuse users
about the source, sponsorship, or status of your work. For example, you may not:

- Distribute a modified version of the software as "Circus Chief" without
  making it clear that it is a modified, unofficial build.
- Use the protected marks as the primary name of your fork, package, add-on,
  hosted service, or company.
- Use the Circus Chief logo or trade dress for a competing or unofficial
  product in a way that suggests official status.
- Register domains, social media accounts, package names, app store listings,
  or paid advertisements that imply official ownership or endorsement.
- Use "Circus Time" for an unofficial recurring-task add-on, license system,
  store listing, or related commercial product.

## Forks and Modified Versions

Forks and modified versions are allowed under the software license, but they
must be branded clearly enough that users can tell they are not the official
Circus Chief distribution.

Acceptable descriptions include phrases such as:

- "Based on Circus Chief"
- "A modified build of Circus Chief"
- "Compatible with Circus Chief"

Avoid names, logos, and UI branding that make the fork appear to be the
official Circus Chief product.

## Add-ons and Integrations

You may describe an add-on or integration as compatible with Circus Chief if
that statement is accurate. You may not name, brand, or market an add-on in a
way that suggests it is official unless you have written permission.

Circus Time is reserved for the official recurring-tasks add-on.

For permission requests or questions about this policy, contact the project
maintainers through the repository's issue tracker or other published project
